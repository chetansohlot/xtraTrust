What is Health Insurance?

Health insurance, also known as a medical insurance plan or policy, is a crucial financial tool that covers medical expenses for injuries, illnesses, and more. From basic hospitalization coverage to comprehensive policies with added benefits like preventive healthcare and death benefits, there’s a wide range of options available.

Types of Health Insurance Plans

Individual Health Insurance

It provides coverage for a single individual under the health insurance policy, covering medical expenses such as hospitalization, injuries, room rent, and more.

Family Floater Health Insurance

This policy covers an entire family of two or more members under a single health insurance plan. The coverage can be utilized by one or all insured members, offering a cost-effective alternative to individual health insurance policies, as the premium is comparatively lower.

Group Health Insurance

A group policy is specifically designed for corporates, covering employees working together. It is offered to employees at a significantly lower premium cost.

Benefits of Health Insurance

Financial Security

Facing a critical illness or major accident could potentially erase your lifetime of hard-earned savings. Health insurance serves as a crucial shield, safeguarding your finances by offering vital financial support during emergencies.

Mental Peace

Health insurance ensures you won’t have to stress about covering hospital bills during emergencies like accidents or illnesses. It provides financial security, giving you peace of mind for the future

Covers Both Illness & Accident

Health insurance covers not only accident-related treatments but also critical illnesses like cancer, heart attack, paralysis, and more.

Covers Existing Diseases

Pre-existing diseases at the time of purchasing the policy come with a waiting period of 1-4 years. However, you can rest assured that they will be covered once the waiting period ends.

Cashless Treatment

Health insurance offers cashless treatment at network hospitals, allowing you to receive care without paying upfront. The insurance company directly settles the hospital bills on your behalf.

Tax Benefit

The premium paid for health insurance is eligible for tax deduction under Section 80D, allowing you to save on taxes. You can claim a deduction of up to ₹75,000 in a financial year.
